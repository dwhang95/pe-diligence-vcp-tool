# LinkedIn Post 01 — PE Value Creation: The AI Adoption Gap

**Topic:** AI integration in PE portfolio operations — the middle-market gap
**Date drafted:** 2026-03-08
**Status:** Draft

---

A PE ops friend sent me a stat last week that stopped me cold.

53% of LPs now rank a GP's value creation strategy in their top-five manager selection criteria. Three years ago, that number wasn't even tracked.

He told me what changed: returns from multiple expansion are gone. Leverage is expensive. The firms posting strong numbers right now are the ones actually inside the business — improving pricing, fixing data infrastructure, running AI pilots that move EBITDA.

I've seen it up close. The middle market is littered with portfolio companies sitting on goldmines of operational data they can't use. Not because the technology doesn't exist. Because nobody built the foundation — clean data, the right governance, someone in the seat who knows what to do with it.

I spent two years on exactly that problem at a middle-market firm. The hardest part wasn't building the tool. It was convincing a management team that their messy spreadsheets were the first thing we had to fix.

The firms winning in 2026 aren't the ones with the fanciest AI. They're the ones doing the unglamorous work first.

That gap — between "we're doing AI" and actually extracting value from it — is the biggest opportunity in PE operations right now.
