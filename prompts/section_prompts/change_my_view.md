# Prompt: What Would Change My View

Generate the "What Would Change My View" section for the PE ops diligence brief.

## Input Context
- Company: {company_name}
- Description: {description}
- Industry: {industry}
- EV range: {ev_range}
- Context notes: {context_notes}
- Research findings: {research_summary}
- Executive summary: {exec_summary_text}
- Risk flags: {risk_flags_summary}
- Value creation opportunities: {value_creation_summary}

## Instructions

This section is the honest IC-level perspective: what would make you more excited or more concerned about this deal.
Do NOT write a balanced summary. Write from a point of view. These are not hedges — they are the explicit conditions that would change your conviction.

**Upgrade Triggers (3–5 items)**
Conditions that, if confirmed in diligence, would make this deal materially more attractive.
These should be specific and falsifiable — not generic ("management team is good") but concrete ("EBITDA margins are understated by 200–300bps due to owner perks and non-recurring costs that are clearly documentable in the P&L").

Examples of well-framed upgrade triggers:
- "Customer concentration is 35% top-3 but all three are on multi-year contracts with auto-renewal — concentration is offset by stickiness"
- "ERP is old but a modern implementation was scoped and budgeted pre-COVID — the work is ready to restart with 6-month payback on ops efficiency"
- "Second-generation owner willing to stay in a minority role for 2 years with meaningful rollover — limits key-man risk"

**Downgrade Triggers (3–5 items)**
Conditions that, if confirmed in diligence, would kill or materially impair the thesis.
Again, be specific and falsifiable. These are deal-breaker risks that deserve an honest statement, not a disclaimer.

Examples of well-framed downgrade triggers:
- "Customer concentration is sticky because pricing is embedded in a long-term contract that also caps margin — we can't expand EBITDA without renegotiation risk"
- "The ERP is deeply customized and the only person who knows it is leaving — a rip-and-replace would cost $2M+ and take 18 months, wiping out the working capital unlock thesis"
- "Revenue growth is entirely attributable to one new customer added in 2023 — stripping that account out, the business is declining"

## Format

Write as two clearly labeled sections:

### Upgrade Triggers
1. [Trigger] — [What it means for the thesis if confirmed true]
2. ...

### Downgrade Triggers
1. [Trigger] — [What it means for the thesis if confirmed true]
2. ...

Be direct. Use PE ops vocabulary. No hedging. These are conviction signals, not risks and mitigants.
Length: 300–500 words total.
